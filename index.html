About Me

I am a PhD researcher in environmental toxicology with a strong focus on ecotoxicology, zebrafish toxicology, and alternative methods for chemical safety assessment.

My research combines computational toxicology, molecular interpretation, and in vivo models to evaluate toxic effects of environmental contaminants and plastic additives.

Zebrafish Assays

Experimental screening to connect chemical exposure with organism-level developmental and functional endpoints.

Development

Hatching, malformations, edema, growth, and survival endpoints.

Cardiotoxicity

Heart rate and visible cardiac phenotypes during early development.

Neuroactivity

Behavioral response patterns and potential neuroactive signatures.

Interpretation

Phenotype profiles linked to toxic ratios and mechanism-of-action hypotheses.

Toxicity Models

I use baseline toxicity prediction and toxic-ratio analysis to distinguish expected narcotic toxicity from potential specific modes of action.

Model-to-assay logic

Predicted baseline toxicity → observed zebrafish effect concentration → toxic ratio → candidate MOA grouping and prioritization.
